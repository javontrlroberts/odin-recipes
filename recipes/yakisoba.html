HAMBURGER YAKISOBA

Hamburger Yakisoba (Ground Beef)

Hamburger Yakisoba is a popular variation of the classic Japanese stir-fried noodle dish. It emerged in the post-World War II era, when flour was scarce and resourceful cooks incorporated readily available ground beef. This hearty and flavorful combination quickly gained traction, becoming a staple in homes and restaurants alike.

Today, Hamburger Yakisoba remains a beloved comfort food for families and consumers of all ages. Its versatility allows for customization with various vegetables and proteins, making it a satisfying and adaptable meal option.

Ingredients

  • 1 package spaghetti noodles
  • Water, boiling
  • Salt
  • 1 pound ground beef, 90% lean
  • 1 green pepper, fresh, julienned
  • 1 onion, fresh, chopped
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • Salt
  • 1 tablespoon garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Water
  • Green onions, fresh, sliced

Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted water until tender, 10 to 12 minutes.
  2. Cook beef in a steam-jacketed kettle or stockpot until the beef loses its pink color, stirring to break apart. Drain and skim off excess fat.
  3. Combine beef with onions, peppers, soy sauce, salt, garlic powder, ginger, and pepper. Cook until onions are tender, about 10 minutes. CCP: Internal temperature must reach 155 F. or higher for 15 seconds.
  4. Add water, green onions, and spaghetti. Mix thoroughly.
  5. Heat to serving temperature. CCP: Internal temperature must reach 155 F. or higher for 15 seconds.
  6. Hold for service at 140 F. or higher.
